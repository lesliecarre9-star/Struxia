The scenarios nobody talks about
What happens if things go wrong?
Most platforms hope you never ask this question. We built Struxia around it. Here is exactly what happens in every bad scenario — no vague promises.
🚪
The contractor disappears mid-project
You've paid milestone 1. Work has started. Then the builder goes silent.
1All unreleased milestone funds are frozen immediately — the builder cannot access them.
2Struxia reviews work completed to date using progress photos and milestone records.
3After 10 business days, unreleased funds are returned to you.
4Builder's account suspended. Struxia Score permanently flagged.
You're protected
Funds returned
🔨
The work is poor quality
The builder marks a milestone complete. You inspect — it doesn't match what was agreed.
1Do not approve. You are never required to release payment if unsatisfied.
2Click "Raise a concern" — Struxia assembles the full record: contract, photos, messages, milestone description.
3Struxia proposes a resolution within 10 business days: remedial work, partial release, or refund.
4Still unresolved: ADR Institute of Canada mediates. Their decision is binding.
Don't approve
Dispute within 10 days
📅
The timeline is missed
The agreed completion date passes. The builder keeps asking for more time.
1The signed contract includes the agreed timeline. A delay is a contract deviation — on record.
2Any extension requires a written change order approved by you. You can decline.
3Final payment stays locked until work is complete and you approve. You are never forced to pay for unfinished work.
On record
Change order required
💬
The builder asks you to pay outside the platform
"Can you e-transfer me directly? Cash discount." This is the most common renovation scam.
⚠Decline immediately. Any payment outside Struxia escrow has zero protection — no dispute process, no recovery.
1Report it via "Raise a concern." Off-platform payment requests are a platform violation.
2First offence: formal warning. Repeat: permanent removal from Struxia.
Never pay outside
Report immediately
📋
The builder wants to add more work and charge more
"We opened the wall and it's going to cost $3,000 more." Scope creep is the #1 cause of renovation cost overruns.
1No extra work begins until a written change order is submitted through Struxia and approved by you.
2Change order specifies exactly: what changed, the new cost, updated milestones. All documented.
3You can decline any change order. Your original scope and price remain your contract.
You decide
